Universal Music Group
The Project aimed to re-design Affiliate marketing platform to optimize online presence and brand image. Nowadays, music production and target audience has changed in the world. This project was initiated based on the shift of UMG digital business strategy instead of the traditional music production website model.
Client Universal Music Group HK
Scope Web Design
Role UX/UI Design

The Objective

UMG website urgently needs revamping that use of site over 5 year rule why itʼs so important to have a new design. Thus, a website redesign need to take place to align website with the expectations of new user groups, that will improve user engagement and trust also helped thier digital Marketing strategy. Primary focus has seen more return visits, brand supporting, audience interaction and increased album revenue through the new affiliate platform.

The Affiliate marketing relationship of UMG: 

  • UMG as a affiliate creator/retailer who provide product and content
  • Our approach as affiliate marketer provide platform and create product tracking links, commonly known as affiliate links in productsʼ detail page will direct consumers to UMGʼs official payment website (UShop.com) to checkout.

My Role

I acted as the lead designer for both UX and UI. Working with the client marketing team and communication with our developers to ensure the best outcome.

THE GOALS OF THE PROJECT:

  • Engaging content to keep visitors as long as possible
  • Avoid too much content cluttering up pages, Make it easy for people to explore
  • Keep it simple and this will help design feel structured. Each page that should be about one subject only
  • Embedded social feed wall on website can help the client no need publish a new content all the time to have look fresh.
  • A responsive design was needed

Process

Our Process started off with information architecture, after which we sketched out low and high-fidelity sketches. After a round of usability testing, we refined our designs to their final version.

  • Sketches

    Quick grab of my sketch book

  • Low-Fi Prototypes

    Post client feedback, the visuals (layout, placement of assets) were iterated on.

  • Mockups

    A considerable amount of UI redesign was required. I created mockups in PS, then exported out for easier communication with developers.

Establishing a visual identity.

With simplicity at the core of the user experience, we brought the site to life with rich content and detailed micro-interactions that inspire users. We wanted to create a site that broke the grid in interesting ways and felt uniform across all the different components and browser sizes. It was important for the site to not only be useful in the information it provides, but also be an enjoyable and aesthetically pleasing experience for the users.

Building out the design system.

The design process involved an iterative process creating a component library which broke the site down into smaller, reusable chunks for the dev team to create. They are flexible for new locations as the brand grows and also provide a highly efficient way to build the site.
